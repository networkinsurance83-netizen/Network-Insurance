Learning center preview

Plain-language insurance education.

Learn the terms and tradeoffs behind life insurance, health insurance, annuities, and supplemental insurance. Official carrier and policy documents control final details.

Premium

The amount paid each month to keep coverage active.

Deductible

What you may pay before the plan starts sharing certain covered costs.

Copayment

A flat amount you may pay for a covered visit, service, or prescription.

Coinsurance

A percentage of covered costs you may pay after meeting a deductible.

Out-of-pocket maximum

A yearly limit on certain covered in-network costs, with exceptions.

Provider network

The doctors, hospitals, and facilities contracted with a plan.

Prescription formulary

The plan's covered drug list and cost tiers.

Preventive care

Certain screenings and services that may be covered in-network under plan rules.

Term life insurance

Coverage for a selected period, often used for temporary protection needs.

Final expense insurance

Life coverage often designed to help with funeral and end-of-life costs.

Accident coverage

Supplemental benefits that may help with certain covered accidental injuries.

Critical illness coverage

Supplemental benefits that may pay for specific covered illnesses under policy rules.

Fixed annuity

A contract that may provide credited interest and optional income features, subject to carrier terms.

Indexed annuity

Crediting may be linked to an index formula, but it is not a direct stock-market investment.

Surrender period

A period when withdrawals above allowed amounts may trigger surrender charges.

Common questions before a coverage review.

What is a coverage review?

Can an advisor help with Marketplace coverage?

An advisor can help review Marketplace questions when appropriate. Eligibility and financial assistance depend on official rules and household information.

Can an advisor review my current plan?

Yes, you can ask questions about costs, networks, prescriptions, and plan structure. Official documents control the final plan details.

Is approval guaranteed?

No. A review does not guarantee approval, eligibility, savings, coverage, or provider access.

Can I keep my current doctor?

Provider networks vary and can change. Always verify with the plan, provider, and official plan documents.

Is life insurance approval guaranteed?

No. Life insurance pricing and approval can depend on underwriting, age, health questions, product type, carrier rules, and other factors.

Does supplemental coverage replace health insurance?

Usually no. Supplemental products may help with specific costs or events, but they are not comprehensive major medical coverage unless specifically designed and approved as such.

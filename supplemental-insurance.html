Supplemental insurance

Learn how supplemental coverage may fit around other protection.

Supplemental insurance may help with specific costs or events, but it is not a replacement for comprehensive major medical insurance unless a product is specifically designed and approved as such.

Coverage categories

Different products address different risks.

Benefits, exclusions, waiting periods, limitations, underwriting, and eligibility vary by policy and carrier.

Dental Coverage

May help with routine dental care or covered procedures, subject to networks, waiting periods, annual maximums, and plan rules.

Vision Coverage

May help with exams, lenses, frames, or contacts depending on policy terms and provider networks.

Accident Coverage

May pay benefits for specific covered accidental injuries under the policy.

Critical Illness Coverage

May pay benefits for certain covered illnesses if policy conditions are met.

Hospital Indemnity

May pay a fixed benefit related to covered hospital stays or services under policy terms.

Disability Income Protection

May replace part of income after a qualifying disability, subject to elimination periods, benefit periods, definitions, and exclusions.

Review the limitations before buying.

Supplemental policies can be useful when understood clearly. They may have fixed benefits, exclusions, waiting periods, benefit caps, and specific triggers. Do not treat fixed-indemnity products as comprehensive health insurance.
